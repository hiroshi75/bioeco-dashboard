# Cover Letter — Paper 1

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"Species richness masks biotic homogenization in depopulating Japanese satoyama: A multi-taxon functional analysis"** for consideration in the *Journal of Applied Ecology*.

Rural depopulation and farmland abandonment are accelerating across Japan and many other developed nations, yet the ecological consequences remain poorly quantified. Using 15 years of standardized monitoring data from 158 satoyama sites and five taxonomic groups (~33,000 records), we demonstrate that conventional species richness indicators fail to detect ongoing ecological change—a 'species richness paradox' in which none of 40 tests were significant. In contrast, functional indicators reveal that non-native plant proportions are increasing significantly across 62% of sites (d = 0.419, p < 0.001), indicating pervasive biotic homogenization.

We believe this work makes three contributions relevant to *Journal of Applied Ecology*:

1. **Multi-taxon evidence for the insensitivity of species richness indicators** in depopulating landscapes, with direct implications for Japan's National Biodiversity Strategy and the CBD Global Biodiversity Framework;

2. **Methodological transparency**, including the discovery and honest reporting of a baseline temperature confound in community temperature index (CTI) analysis, resolved through propensity score matching—a technique rarely applied in biodiversity monitoring;

3. **A complete audit of 93 statistical tests** with Benjamini-Hochberg correction at two family levels, and clear distinction between confirmatory and exploratory analyses, contributing to open science practices in applied ecology.

Our analysis builds upon and differentiates from recent species-level studies (Katayama et al. 2024 *Conservation Biology*; Uchida et al. 2025 *Nature Sustainability*) by applying community-level functional indicators that these prior works did not employ.

This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission.
