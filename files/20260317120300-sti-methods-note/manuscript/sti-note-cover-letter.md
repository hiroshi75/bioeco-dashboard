# Cover Letter — STI Methods Note

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript "How reliable is your Community Temperature Index? A practical framework for STI quality control in climate debt assessments" for consideration as an Application Note in Methods in Ecology and Evolution.

**Why this paper matters**: The Community Temperature Index (CTI) is one of the most widely used metrics in climate change ecology, employed in over 200 published studies. Yet its reliability depends entirely on the quality of underlying Species Temperature Index (STI) values — a dependency that has received surprisingly little systematic attention. We demonstrate through a case study of Japanese butterfly and bird communities that CTI conclusions can reverse entirely depending on STI source, taxonomic match rate, and spatial resolution.

**Key findings**:
- Across six independent analyses spanning two taxa (butterflies, birds) and two analytical frameworks (temporal CTI trends, spatial RDD), STI quality determined the direction and significance of CTI conclusions
- CTI trends reversed from significant decrease (-0.88 C/decade) to non-significant increase (+0.30 C/decade) as STI match rate improved from 13% to 100%
- A site-specific STI match rate of 23% (below our proposed 50% prohibition threshold) produced a completely reversed CTI-RDD conclusion in bird communities
- We propose a practical STI Quality Framework comprising confidence flags, match-rate thresholds, and mandatory sensitivity analysis

**Why MEE**: This work provides a practical methodological framework directly applicable to the large community of CTI researchers. The STI Quality Framework — comprising three-tier confidence flags, match-rate thresholds (<50% prohibition, 50-80% sensitivity analysis required, >80% stable), and site-specific quality checks — addresses a systematic blind spot in current CTI practice. The case study approach, with six documented artifacts, provides compelling evidence for adopting these standards.

**Data and reproducibility**: All STI datasets and analysis code will be deposited in a public GitHub repository with a Zenodo DOI upon acceptance.

This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere.
